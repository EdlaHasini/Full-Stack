Methodology

From leaf image
to diagnosis

The detection pipeline extracts color, texture, and shape features from high-resolution rice leaf photographs, then runs them through trained ML classifiers.

01
📷
Image Capture
High-resolution paddy leaf images captured in various lighting conditions and growth stages — both healthy and infected leaves.
02
⚙️
Preprocessing
Images are resized and normalised to [0, 1] pixel range. RGB channels are extracted for color feature computation.
03
🔬
Feature Extraction
Color histograms, GLCM texture metrics (contrast, correlation, energy), Local Binary Patterns, and Canny edge shapes are computed.
04
🤖
ML Classification
SVM with RBF kernel, 100-tree Random Forest, KNN, and GradientBoostingClassifier are each evaluated on the feature set.
05
📊
Evaluation
Each model is scored on accuracy, precision, recall, and F1-score to determine the most reliable BLB detection algorithm.
06
🌾
Field Action
Diagnosis results guide disease management — targeted treatment, resistant variety selection, drainage optimisation, and monitoring.

About This Research

Bacterial Leaf Blight
& Global Food Security

Bacterial Leaf Blight (BLB), caused by Xanthomonas oryzae pv. oryzae, is one of the most destructive diseases affecting rice crops worldwide. It causes significant yield losses and threatens global food security, particularly in South and Southeast Asia.

Traditional detection methods — manual inspection by agronomists — are labour-intensive, inconsistent, and prone to error. Early detection is critical: BLB spreads rapidly under humid conditions and can devastate an entire field within weeks if left untreated.

This research demonstrates that machine learning models trained on image features — particularly color percentages and plant age — can reliably detect BLB, opening a path toward automated, real-time field monitoring integrated with IoT devices and mobile applications.

The study was published at the 2024 IEEE International Conference on Inventive Systems and Control (ICISC), conducted at Annamalai University, Chidambaram, Tamil Nadu.
